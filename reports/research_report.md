# Care Transition Analytics — Research Report

## 1. Problem Statement

The Unaccompanied Children (UAC) care pipeline spans multiple federal agencies and custody stages. Operational leaders need timely visibility into transfer efficiency, placement outcomes, backlog accumulation, and future capacity requirements. This platform provides automated measurement, detection, forecasting, and recommendation capabilities across the full care transition lifecycle.

## 2. Methodology

### Data Pipeline
1. **Load** — CSV ingestion with column standardization
2. **Validate** — Missing values, duplicates, negative entries
3. **Clean** — Datetime parsing, chronological sort, forward-fill, invalid record removal
4. **Feature Engineering** — Derived KPIs and rolling metrics (7/14/30-day)
5. **Analytics** — Bottleneck rules, Isolation Forest anomalies, Prophet forecasting
6. **Visualization** — Interactive Streamlit dashboard with Plotly charts

### Analytical Approach
- **Descriptive:** Time-series trends, monthly aggregates, correlation analysis
- **Diagnostic:** Rule-based bottleneck detection with severity scoring
- **Predictive:** Prophet time-series forecasting with confidence intervals
- **Prescriptive:** Recommendation engine mapping KPI degradation to operational actions

## 3. KPI Definitions

| KPI | Definition | Threshold |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Transfer Efficiency | Transfers ÷ CBP Custody | Alert < 50% |
| Discharge Effectiveness | Discharges ÷ HHS Population | Alert < 40% |
| Pipeline Throughput | Discharges ÷ Apprehensions | Alert < historical avg |
| Daily Backlog | Apprehensions − Discharges | 7-day positive streak |
| Cumulative Backlog | Σ daily backlog | Trend monitoring |
| Outcome Stability | 7-day rolling σ of discharge effectiveness | Volatility indicator |

## 4. EDA Findings

Key exploratory patterns observed in the HHS UAC dataset:

- **Apprehension volatility** — Daily intake varies significantly, driving downstream load fluctuations
- **HHS population persistence** — HHS care population remains the largest pipeline stage by volume
- **Transfer-discharge coupling** — Transfer volumes and discharge rates show correlated movement
- **Backlog accumulation** — Cumulative backlog trends reveal periods of sustained operational pressure
- **Seasonal patterns** — Weekly seasonality detected in transfer and discharge volumes (captured by Prophet)

## 5. Forecast Results

Prophet models generate 30-day forecasts for:
- Future apprehensions
- Future transfers
- Future HHS population
- Future discharges

Each forecast includes:
- Point estimate (`yhat`)
- 80% confidence interval (`yhat_lower`, `yhat_upper`)
- Trend direction indicator

Forecasts support proactive capacity planning for shelter resources and case management staffing.

## 6. Recommendations

### Operational
1. Monitor transfer efficiency daily against the 50% threshold
2. Review sponsor placement workflows when discharge effectiveness drops below 40%
3. Activate surge protocols when cumulative backlog shows sustained growth

### Analytical
1. Refresh forecasts weekly with latest operational data
2. Investigate anomaly-flagged dates for external event correlation
3. Extend bottleneck rules with agency-specific SLA thresholds as data matures

### Strategic
1. Integrate real-time data feeds for sub-daily monitoring
2. Add demographic and regional segmentation for targeted interventions
3. Deploy automated alert notifications to operations leadership
